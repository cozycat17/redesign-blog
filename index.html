Usability issues

In general, the Brown MMA website does not present a digestible hierarchy of information. The things that people need to know are hidden. Elsewhere, key space is wasted. This detracts from both the learnability and efficiency of the website.

To identify the information which should be prioritized I created two personas:

Nervous Nelson has never attended a club meeting. He is trying to decide whether to go to a meeting. He is looking for basic information about the club. He also wants to know when and where meetings are if he decides to go.

He thinks:
  • I wonder if I can just show up to this club? What would I need to wear and bring?
  • Is this club for beginners or is it tryout based?
  • When and where would I go to attend a meeting?

Avid August is a dedicated member of the club. He knows when regular practice times are and the vibe of each session. His goal using the website is to see when one time events are or to get the contact information of the club members.

He thinks:
  • When and where was that UFC watch party again? What day and time is the tournament?
  • I need to contact the club leader because I am going to be late to the tournament
Using these personas, I found three main usability issues with the current website:
  1. Hidden date and time details
  2. Lack of information for first time/prospective attendees
  3. Poor accessibility
1. Hidden date and time details

Both experienced and new users rely on the website for basic time and place information. However, the home screen does not fill this need. In fact, about 90% of the screen is occupied by a generic Brown bear picture.

Currently, date/time information is
  1. Hidden in the middle of a paragraph
  2. Excludes the date and time for the kind of one time events that Avid August (experienced member) is interested in.

This can be seen below, underlined in white.

2. Lack of information for first time/prospective attendees

The only place on the entire website that this information is partially given is in a small blurb on the bottom of the home page. However, the paragraph does not directly answer questions and leaves a lot unknown. This information could be the difference between Nervous Nelson checking out a meeting or not.
